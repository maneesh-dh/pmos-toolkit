Problem

The /survey-design skill produced a fieldable survey end-to-end on its first real run, but an experienced PM had to manually surface six structural problems the built-in Phase-4 reviewer never caught — including a question whose answers were entirely predictable ("everybody is going to say they went to the analytics team — what's the signal?") that should have been killed before fielding. The reviewer checks methodology and an anti-pattern catalog; it does not check whether each question is load-bearing for the stated research goal, whether its likely answers carry signal, or whether its framing matches the actual scope of the thing being researched — the checks a product reviewer leads with. Separately: the generated intro block is functional-not-persuasive (no reason for a busy respondent to spend the time); the skill silently degrades when shared substrate assets are missing instead of failing loud; cosmetic-only reviewer findings crowd the user-facing dispositions batch; and there is no question type for a "metrics by cadence"–style item (one labeled input per period), forcing an ad-hoc schema extension mid-run.

Who experiences this?

Two roles. (1) Survey authors using /survey-design — PMs, researchers, founders — who need the reviewer to catch product-fit problems, not just methodology nits, and who want a persuasive intro and a clean dispositions flow. (2) Survey respondents — the audience of the generated survey — who need a reason to participate (the intro nudge) and questions worth answering.

Why now?

First production use of the skill (shipped v2.36.0). The retro from that session is the trigger; the gaps are concrete and reproduced, not speculative.

Goals & Non-Goals

Goals

  • (F1) The Phase-4 reviewer leads with product-fit, not methodology. Both eval artifacts surface product-fit findings first — question-eval.md with a per-question binary PASS/FAIL on {answers predictable? load-bearing for the stated purpose? framing matches scope?}, survey-eval.md with an overall "research-goal coverage / product fit" section — measured by: the rubric and contract are in SKILL.md + reference/survey-best-practices.md, the generator self-checks against it, and a fielded survey's known-predictable / non-load-bearing questions are flagged for kill/rewrite ahead of methodology findings.
  • (F3) The skill fails loud, not degraded, on missing substrate assets. If a cp -n source asset is absent, the skill aborts with a clear reinstall message — measured by: Phases 3.6 and 7 instruct the abort + message; the edge-case index lists it.
  • (F6) A multi_field_open question type exists, with defined export behavior on every platform. Measured by: the type is in the §3.2 enum with a fields array and a §3.3 time-cost constant; reference/platform-export.md maps it natively where supported and to the N-short-answer-items + section-header downgrade where not (the Google Forms pattern); preview.html / survey-preview.js render it.
  • (F7) Cosmetic-only reviewer findings don't crowd the dispositions batch. Measured by: Phases 5 & 6 exclude severity-nit-AND-cosmetic findings from the batched AskUserQuestion and list them in a trailing "Noted, not asked:" line, still recorded in ## Dispositions.
  • (F8) The generated intro block includes a persuasive respondent-motivation line. Measured by: reference/survey-best-practices.md + Phase 3.4 mandate a WIIFM line in intro.text; the §3.1 example models it; the reviewer's intro/consent check flags its absence.

Non-Goals (explicit scope cuts)

  • NOT adding a Google-Forms "Submit greyed out" guard (the setCollectEmail/setLimitOneResponsePerUser footgun) — because the example .gs recipe is already clean; the generator improvised the bad settings in-session, so this is not a skill defect to codify against. (Triage F2, skipped.)
  • NOT adding extra prose about the inline simulation being weaker than the subagent pass — because the existing FR-44 "heuristic stand-in, doesn't replace cognitive interviews / soft launch" note in Phase 6 already covers it. (Triage F4, skipped.)
  • NOT special-casing Phase-1 intake for unreachable external context (e.g. a Notion URL behind a disconnected MCP) — because try→fail→ask is acceptable and this isn't common enough to harden. (Triage F5, skipped.)
  • NOT redesigning the simulated-respondent pass, the export transformers beyond the multi_field_open mapping, or the non-interactive contract — out of scope for this batch.

User Experience Analysis

Motivation

  • Job to be done (author): get a survey that won't waste the respondents' time or come back with un-actionable data — i.e., trust the skill's reviewer the way you'd trust a sharp colleague who reads your draft and says "kill Q2, everyone will say the same thing".
  • Importance/Urgency: high — a badly-scoped or low-signal survey that ships is expensive (respondent goodwill spent, decisions made on noise). The reviewer is the last gate before fielding.
  • Alternatives today: the author manually critiques every question (what happened in the retro) — slow, and only works if the author is themselves an experienced survey reviewer.

  • An author re-runs /survey-design on the retro's original brief and the reviewer itself flags the broad-scope, the redundant confidentiality line, the optional-questions-over-budget, and the predictable Q2 — without the author raising them.

  • A generated survey's intro reads like something a person would actually keep reading.

Solution Direction

All five changes live inside the existing /survey-design skill — no new skill, no new pipeline stage. The shape:

  • F1 — product-fit rubric, folded into the existing eval artifacts. Add a leading section to reference/survey-best-practices.md defining a binary, purpose-contextual rubric: per question — (a) predictable answer? would essentially everyone in the audience pick 1–2 of the same responses, so the question yields no differentiating signal; (b) load-bearing? would dropping this question change what the author learns relative to purpose; (c) framing matches scope? does the question's framing match the actual scope of the product/topic being researched (not broader, not narrower). Plus an overall check: does the question set answer purpose — coverage gaps, redundant items, scope drift. The Phase-4 reviewer prompt + return contract change so question-eval.md leads each ## <question-id> section with a PASS/FAIL "product fit" block (a FAIL marks the question a kill-or-rewrite candidate), surfaced ahead of the methodology/anti-pattern findings; survey-eval.md leads with a "Research-goal coverage / product fit" section before the existing Structure/Length/Mode/Scale/Accessibility/Ethics/Intro sections. Phase 3 generation self-checks every question against the same rubric before committing (analogous to the existing "generate none of the anti-patterns" self-check). The Anti-Patterns list and edge-case index gain corresponding entries.
  • F3 — fail-loud on missing substrate assets. Phases 3.6 and 7: before/at each cp -n, if the source asset is absent, abort with survey-design: missing substrate asset <path> — reinstall the pmos-toolkit plugin rather than producing a degraded artifact. No fallback renderer (assets ship with the repo). Platform-Adaptation notes / edge-case index updated.
  • F6 — multi_field_open type + export downgrade coverage. §3.2 type enum gains multi_field_open with a fields: [{id, label, placeholder}] array (each field a single-line free-text input under a shared stem); §3.3 gains its time-cost constant (≈ open_short per field, i.e. 30s/field, or a tuned value); schema invariants updated as needed; schema_version bumped to 2 (additive, backward-compatible). reference/platform-export.md: define the mapping per platform — native where a platform supports a labeled multi-input group, else downgrade to N open_short items + a section-header explanation (the Google Forms pattern), reflected in the type-mapping tables; the export/README.md template lists the downgrade for affected platforms; the meaning-changing downgrade gets an in-artifact comment. preview.html / assets/survey-preview.js render the type (a labeled input list / small table); survey.html renders it likewise.
  • F7 — cosmetic-finding filter. Phases 5 & 6: a finding is "cosmetic-only" iff severity is nit AND the defect class is a wording/title polish with no methodological or product-fit impact. Such findings are excluded from the batched AskUserQuestion dispositions and listed in a trailing Noted, not asked: line; they are still recorded in the ## Dispositions section of question-eval.md / survey-eval.md. The relevant Anti-Pattern ("don't dump findings as prose") gains a note.
  • F8 — persuasive intro line. reference/survey-best-practices.md intro section + Phase 3.4: the generated intro.text MUST include a persuasive respondent-motivation / WIIFM line — what concretely changes as a result of their answers, audience-specific framing — kept honest (no fake scarcity, no fake urgency, no overclaiming impact). The §3.1 intro.text example is rewritten to model it. The Phase-4 reviewer's intro/consent check (in survey-eval.md) flags its absence.

Release wiring (handled by /complete-dev, not designed here, but must not be broken): minor version bump in both plugin.json manifests kept byte-identical; README row stays under "### Utilities"; non-interactive block stays inlined verbatim; ## /survey-design header in ~/.pmos/learnings.md; CHANGELOG entry; canonical path plugins/pmos-toolkit/skills/survey-design/SKILL.md.

User Journeys

Primary Journey (Happy Path) — author runs /survey-design with the F1 reviewer

  1. Author invokes /survey-design with a research intent; Phases 1–3 resolve variables and the generator builds survey.json. New: during generation each question is self-checked against the product-fit rubric; a question that fails (e.g. its likely answers are obvious given the audience) is reworked or dropped before commit.
  2. Phase 4 dispatches the reviewer. New: the reviewer first scores each question PASS/FAIL on the three product-fit checks and writes the "product fit" block at the top of each ## <id> section in question-eval.md; it writes the "Research-goal coverage / product fit" section at the top of survey-eval.md; then it does the methodology/anti-pattern pass as today.
  3. Phase 5 presents findings. New: product-fit FAILs appear at the top of the dispositions batch, framed as "kill or rewrite Q<id>?" with the rubric reason; cosmetic-only findings are not in the batch — they appear in a trailing "Noted, not asked:" line.
  4. Author dispositions the findings (Fix / Modify / Skip / Defer); the skill mutates survey.json, re-derives the rendered artifacts, records dispositions, commits.
  5. Phases 6–9 proceed as today. The fielded survey's intro carries a persuasive WIIFM line (F8); a multi_field_open question, if present, renders in preview.html and exports correctly per platform (F6).

Alternate Journeys

  • Reviewer finds no product-fit problems: each question's "product fit" block reads PASS on all three checks; survey-eval.md's coverage section says the set answers purpose with no gaps; the dispositions batch contains only methodology findings (or none). The skill proceeds to simulation without forcing edits — same as the existing "reviewer finds nothing" edge case, just with the product-fit checks included in "nothing".
  • Author keeps a question the reviewer FAILed: the FAIL disposition is recorded as Skip-with-reason in ## Dispositions; the survey is committed with the question; the Phase-9 summary notes the kept-against-advice item (parallel to the existing over-budget handling).
  • Survey uses a multi_field_open question and the chosen export platform doesn't support it natively: the transformer emits N open_short items + a section-header explanation; export/README.md lists the downgrade; an in-artifact comment marks it.

Error Journeys

  • A substrate source asset is missing (e.g. _shared/html-authoring/assets/style.css or survey-design/assets/survey-preview.js not present): the skill aborts at Phase 3.6 / Phase 7 with survey-design: missing substrate asset <path> — reinstall the pmos-toolkit plugin. It does not write a degraded survey.html/preview.html. The author reinstalls and re-runs.
  • Reviewer subagent returns a malformed product-fit block (missing the PASS/FAIL for some question): same handling as the existing per-question count mismatch — re-dispatch once naming the gaps; if still off, surface the gap and proceed without auto-applying anything.

Design Decisions

#DecisionOptions ConsideredRationale
D1Product-fit checks are folded into the existing survey-eval.md / question-eval.md artifacts, not a separate sub-pass with its own files.(a) separate product-fit.md artifact + extra reviewer dispatch; (b) fold into the two existing eval artifacts as their leading section(b) — user-confirmed. One reviewer dispatch, one place to look; the product-fit findings lead so they're seen first. Avoids a third eval file and a second subagent round-trip.
D2The product-fit checks are binary PASS/FAIL per question, contextual to purpose/audience — not a numeric score.(a) 1–5 scores per check; (b) binary PASS/FAIL with a one-line reason(b) — matches the user's stated objective ("a proper binary pass/fail eval criteria"); a FAIL maps cleanly to a "kill or rewrite" disposition; scores invite haggling over a 3 vs a 4.
D3A product-fit FAIL is surfaced as a kill/rewrite candidate for the author to disposition — it does not auto-remove the question.(a) auto-drop FAILed questions; (b) surface as the top finding, author decides(b) — consistent with every other reviewer finding in the skill (the author always dispositions); auto-dropping would violate the "one good pass, let the user decide" anti-pattern.
D4On a missing substrate asset the skill aborts; it does not ship a self-contained fallback renderer.(a) abort with a reinstall message; (b) inline a fallback renderer used when assets are absent(a) — the assets ship with the repo; a missing asset means a broken install, which the author should fix, not work around silently. A fallback renderer is a second code path to maintain for a case that shouldn't occur.
D5multi_field_open is added to the schema with a defined per-platform downgrade, rather than left as an ad-hoc extension or omitted.(a) omit it (author keeps extending ad-hoc); (b) add it with native-or-downgrade export mapping(b) — user wants it, but explicitly flagged the export concern; adding it without defining the downgrade would push the ad-hoc problem into the export phase. schema_version bump to 2 keeps it backward-compatible.
D6"Cosmetic-only" = severity nit AND a wording/title polish with no methodological or product-fit impact; such findings are listed, not asked.(a) drop cosmetic findings entirely; (b) keep them in the batch but sort last; (c) list them in a trailing line, still recorded in ## Dispositions(c) — the author still sees them (can act later) but isn't asked to disposition each; dropping them loses information, sorting-last still costs a click each.

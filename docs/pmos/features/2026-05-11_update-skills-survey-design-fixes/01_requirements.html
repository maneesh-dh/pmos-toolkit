Date: 2026-05-11
Last updated: 2026-05-11
Status: Draft
Tier: 3 — Feature (skill change; touches the reviewer rubric and the authoritative schema)
Source: /update-skills triage at docs/pmos/features/2026-05-11_update-skills-survey-design-fixes/00_triage.md — 5 approved changes (F1, F3, F6, F7, F8) to the /survey-design skill.

Problem

The /survey-design skill produced a fieldable survey end-to-end on its first real run, but an experienced PM had to manually surface six structural problems the built-in Phase-4 reviewer never caught — including a question whose answers were entirely predictable ("everybody is going to say they went to the analytics team — what's the signal?") that should have been killed before fielding. The reviewer checks methodology and an anti-pattern catalog; it does not check whether each question is load-bearing for the stated research goal, whether its likely answers carry signal, or whether its framing matches the actual scope of the thing being researched — the checks a product reviewer leads with. Separately: the generated intro block is functional-not-persuasive (no reason for a busy respondent to spend the time); the skill silently degrades when shared substrate assets are missing instead of failing loud; cosmetic-only reviewer findings crowd the user-facing dispositions batch; and there is no question type for a "metrics by cadence"–style item (one labeled input per period), forcing an ad-hoc schema extension mid-run.

Who experiences this?

Two roles. (1) Survey authors using /survey-design — PMs, researchers, founders — who need the reviewer to catch product-fit problems, not just methodology nits, and who want a persuasive intro and a clean dispositions flow. (2) Survey respondents — the audience of the generated survey — who need a reason to participate (the intro nudge) and questions worth answering.

Why now?

First production use of the skill (shipped v2.36.0). The retro from that session is the trigger; the gaps are concrete and reproduced, not speculative.

Goals & Non-Goals

Goals

  • (F1) Phase 4 is a scored generate↔review refinement loop that catches product-fit, not just methodology. The Phase-4 reviewer evaluates only — it returns a weighted 0–100 score across all eval dimensions plus per-dimension findings (including a per-question product-fit verdict) plus recommended edits; it never mutates survey.json. When the exit condition isn't met (a product-fit FAIL or a blocker-severity methodology finding remains) and the iteration cap (2) isn't reached, control returns to the generator, which regenerates the flagged questions incorporating the reviewer's notes, then re-review. On exit, Phase 5 surfaces the residual findings to the author. Measured by: the rubric (the 0–100 dimensions + the per-question product-fit checks) lives in reference/survey-best-practices.md; question-eval.md leads each ## <id> section with the product-fit verdict and carries a per-iteration changelog; survey-eval.md leads with the score + a "research-goal coverage / product fit" section; a re-run on the retro's original brief has the loop flag (and rewrite or surface) the predictable / non-load-bearing / scope-mismatched questions on its own.
  • (F1, cont.) The product-fit checks are binary, contextual, and evidence-backed. Per question: (a) predictable answer? — closed-ended: the reviewer writes the predicted answer distribution, FAIL iff 1–2 options absorb ~80%+; open-ended: yes/no on whether multiple distinct high-signal answers are realistically expected, FAIL iff it collapses to 1–2 predictable themes; (b) load-bearing? — would dropping it change what the author learns relative to purpose; (c) framing matches scope? — is the question framed at the actual scope of the topic being researched (not broader, not narrower). The written prediction is the evidence and goes in the product-fit block.
  • (F3) The skill fails loud on missing hard-required substrate assets, and warn-degrades on convenience ones. Missing survey-preview.js / style.css / viewer.js (load-bearing for preview.html / survey.html) ⇒ abort with survey-design: missing substrate asset <path> — reinstall the pmos-toolkit plugin; missing serve.js / html-to-md.js / the turndown bundle (convenience) ⇒ warn and skip that extra (no preview server / no .md sidecar), continue. Measured by: Phases 3.6 and 7 implement the two-tier rule; the edge-case index lists both branches.
  • (F6) A multi_field_open question type exists, with defined export behavior on every platform. Measured by: the type is in the §3.2 enum with a fields array and a §3.3 time-cost constant; schema_version is bumped to 2; reference/platform-export.md maps it natively where supported and to the N-short-answer-items + section-header downgrade where not (the Google Forms pattern); preview.html / survey-preview.js render it.
  • (F7) Cosmetic-only reviewer findings don't crowd the dispositions batch. Measured by: Phases 5 & 6 exclude severity-nit-AND-cosmetic findings from the batched AskUserQuestion and list them in a trailing "Noted, not asked:" line, still recorded in ## Dispositions.
  • (F8) The generated intro block includes a persuasive respondent-motivation line built from author-supplied impact. Measured by: Phase 2 intake gains a variable — what happens to the responses / what does the audience get; reference/survey-best-practices.md + Phase 3.4 mandate a WIIFM line in intro.text derived from it (or, if unsupplied, a benefit-framed restatement of purpose — never a fabricated impact claim); the §3.1 example models it; the reviewer's intro/consent check flags its absence.

Non-Goals (explicit scope cuts)

  • NOT adding a Google-Forms "Submit greyed out" guard (the setCollectEmail/setLimitOneResponsePerUser footgun) — because the example .gs recipe is already clean; the generator improvised the bad settings in-session, so this is not a skill defect to codify against. (Triage F2, skipped.)
  • NOT adding extra prose about the inline simulation being weaker than the subagent pass — because the existing FR-44 "heuristic stand-in, doesn't replace cognitive interviews / soft launch" note in Phase 6 already covers it. (Triage F4, skipped.)
  • NOT special-casing Phase-1 intake for unreachable external context (e.g. a Notion URL behind a disconnected MCP) — because try→fail→ask is acceptable and this isn't common enough to harden. (Triage F5, skipped.)
  • NOT redesigning the simulated-respondent pass, the export transformers beyond the multi_field_open mapping, or the non-interactive contract — out of scope for this batch.

User Experience Analysis

Motivation

  • Job to be done (author): get a survey that won't waste the respondents' time or come back with un-actionable data — i.e., trust the skill's reviewer the way you'd trust a sharp colleague who reads your draft and says "kill Q2, everyone will say the same thing".
  • Importance/Urgency: high — a badly-scoped or low-signal survey that ships is expensive (respondent goodwill spent, decisions made on noise). The reviewer is the last gate before fielding.
  • Alternatives today: the author manually critiques every question (what happened in the retro) — slow, and only works if the author is themselves an experienced survey reviewer.

Satisfaction Signals

  • An author re-runs /survey-design on the retro's original brief and the reviewer itself flags the broad-scope, the redundant confidentiality line, the optional-questions-over-budget, and the predictable Q2 — without the author raising them.
  • The dispositions batch shown to the author contains only findings worth a decision; cosmetic items appear once, listed, not asked.
  • A generated survey's intro reads like something a person would actually keep reading.

Solution Direction

All five changes live inside the existing /survey-design skill — no new skill, no new pipeline stage. The shape:

  • F1 — Phase 4 becomes a scored generate↔review refinement loop; product-fit checks live in the reviewer. The reviewer (Phase 4, still a fresh-context subagent) evaluates only: it returns (i) a weighted 0–100 score across all eval dimensions — product-fit, structure/funnel, length-vs-budget, mode fit, scale balance, accessibility, ethics/PII, intro/consent (weights ⇒ /spec); (ii) per-dimension findings, each still tagged blocker/should-fix/nit; (iii) a per-question product-fit verdict on the three binary checks below; and (iv) recommended edits (rewritten stems, "kill Q<id>", added opt-outs). It does not mutate survey.json. The loop: Phase 3 generates → Phase 4 reviews → if a product-fit FAIL or a blocker-severity methodology finding remains AND iterations < 2, control returns to the generator, which regenerates the flagged questions incorporating the reviewer's recommended edits → re-review → repeat. Exit is categorical, not score-gated (zero product-fit FAILs + zero blockers, or the 2-iteration cap) — the 0–100 score is shown as a progress signal ("72 → 91") and used to prioritize which findings the loop fixes first, but never decides termination (a single LLM-judge score near a hard cutoff would be too flappy to gate on). On exit, Phase 5 surfaces the residual findings to the author for disposition as today, opening with a "what the refinement loop changed" summary (read from the per-iteration changelog in question-eval.md) so the author can revert anything.
    The product-fit rubric (a new leading section in reference/survey-best-practices.md) is binary and purpose-contextual. Per question: (a) predictable answer? — closed-ended: the reviewer writes the predicted answer distribution, FAIL iff 1–2 options would plausibly absorb ~80%+; open-ended: a yes/no judgment on whether multiple distinct high-signal answers are realistically expected, FAIL iff the open question would also collapse to 1–2 predictable themes; (b) load-bearing? — would dropping the question change what the author learns relative to purpose; (c) framing matches scope? — is the question framed at the actual scope of the topic being researched (not broader, not narrower). Plus an overall survey-eval.md check: does the question set answer purpose — coverage gaps, redundant items, scope drift. question-eval.md leads each ## <question-id> section with the product-fit verdict + the written prediction (the evidence) ahead of the methodology/anti-pattern findings; survey-eval.md leads with the score + the "research-goal coverage / product fit" section before the existing Structure/Length/Mode/Scale/Accessibility/Ethics/Intro sections. The old Phase-3 "self-check every question against the rubric before committing" step is removed — that work is now done by the reviewer in the loop. In Phase 5, product-fit FAILs are the top tier of the dispositions batch: one "Kill or rewrite Q<id>? — <rubric reason>" question per FAIL (Kill / Rewrite-as-proposed / Keep-with-reason / Defer), before blocker → should-fix → nit. The Anti-Patterns list and edge-case index gain corresponding entries; the existing "don't run the reviewer in a loop to be thorough" anti-pattern is revised to "the loop is bounded (cap 2, categorical exit) — don't extend it."
  • F3 — fail-loud on missing substrate assets, two-tier. Phases 3.6 and 7: before each cp -n, classify the source asset. Hard-required (survey-preview.js, style.css, viewer.js — load-bearing for the preview.html / survey.html the skill promises): if absent, abort with survey-design: missing substrate asset <path> — reinstall the pmos-toolkit plugin rather than producing a degraded artifact. Convenience (serve.js, html-to-md.js, the turndown bundle): if absent, emit one warning and skip the dependent extra (no preview server / no .md sidecar), continue. No fallback renderer (assets ship with the repo). Platform-Adaptation notes / edge-case index updated with both branches.
  • F6 — multi_field_open type + export downgrade coverage. §3.2 type enum gains multi_field_open with a fields: [{id, label, placeholder}] array (each field a single-line free-text input under a shared stem); §3.3 gains its time-cost constant (≈ open_short per field, i.e. 30s/field, or a tuned value); schema invariants updated as needed; schema_version bumped to 2 (additive, backward-compatible). reference/platform-export.md: define the mapping per platform — native where a platform supports a labeled multi-input group, else downgrade to N open_short items + a section-header explanation (the Google Forms pattern), reflected in the type-mapping tables; the export/README.md template lists the downgrade for affected platforms; the meaning-changing downgrade gets an in-artifact comment. preview.html / assets/survey-preview.js render the type (a labeled input list / small table); survey.html renders it likewise.
  • F7 — cosmetic-finding filter. Phases 5 & 6: a finding is "cosmetic-only" iff severity is nit AND the defect class is a wording/title polish with no methodological or product-fit impact. Such findings are excluded from the batched AskUserQuestion dispositions and listed in a trailing Noted, not asked: line; they are still recorded in the ## Dispositions section of question-eval.md / survey-eval.md. The relevant Anti-Pattern ("don't dump findings as prose") gains a note.
  • F8 — persuasive intro line, sourced from the author. Phase 2 intake gains a variable — what happens to the responses / what does the audience get out of it (free-form; no auto-pickable default; if the author skips it, the generator falls back to a benefit-framed restatement of purpose, never a fabricated impact claim). reference/survey-best-practices.md intro section + Phase 3.4: the generated intro.text MUST include a persuasive respondent-motivation / WIIFM line built from that answer — what concretely changes as a result of their answers, audience-specific framing — kept honest (no fake scarcity, no fake urgency, no overclaiming impact). The §3.1 intro.text example is rewritten to model it. The Phase-4 reviewer's intro/consent check (in survey-eval.md, contributing to the intro/consent score dimension) flags its absence.

Release wiring (handled by /complete-dev, not designed here, but must not be broken): minor version bump in both plugin.json manifests kept byte-identical; README row stays under "### Utilities"; non-interactive block stays inlined verbatim; ## /survey-design header in ~/.pmos/learnings.md; CHANGELOG entry; canonical path plugins/pmos-toolkit/skills/survey-design/SKILL.md.

User Journeys

Primary Journey (Happy Path) — author runs /survey-design with the Phase-4 refinement loop

  1. Author invokes /survey-design with a research intent. Phase 2 intake resolves the design variables, including the new what happens to the responses variable (F8). Phase 3 generation builds the initial survey.json per reference/survey-best-practices.md (no product-fit self-grade — that's the reviewer's job now).
  2. Phase 4, iteration 1: the reviewer subagent (fresh context) evaluates survey.json — returns a 0–100 score, per-dimension findings (each tagged blocker/should-fix/nit), a per-question product-fit verdict (with the written predicted-answer evidence), and recommended edits. It writes survey-eval.md (leads with the score + the research-goal-coverage section) and question-eval.md (each ## <id> leads with the product-fit verdict). It does not touch survey.json.
  3. Some questions FAIL product-fit (e.g. a question whose answers everyone in the audience would give the same way) and there's a methodology blocker; iteration cap (2) not reached → control returns to the generator, which regenerates the flagged questions incorporating the reviewer's recommended edits, logging what changed to the per-iteration changelog in question-eval.md. Phase 4 iteration 2: re-review. Now zero product-fit FAILs and zero blockers remain → exit condition met; the score (a progress signal) has gone, say, 72 → 91.
  4. Phase 5 opens with a "what the refinement loop changed" summary (from the changelog), then presents the residual findings — any remaining should-fix/nit items, plus anything the loop hit the cap on (still-FAILing product-fit questions surface here as "Kill or rewrite Q<id>?" at the top of the batch). Cosmetic-only findings are not in the batch — they appear in a trailing "Noted, not asked:" line. Author dispositions (Fix / Modify / Skip / Defer or, for FAILs, Kill / Rewrite / Keep-with-reason / Defer); the skill mutates survey.json, re-derives the rendered artifacts, records dispositions, commits.
  5. Phases 6–9 proceed as today. The fielded survey's intro carries a persuasive WIIFM line built from the F8 variable; a multi_field_open question, if present, renders in preview.html and exports native-or-downgraded per platform.

Alternate Journeys

  • First review passes the exit condition immediately: iteration 1 returns zero product-fit FAILs and zero blockers → no regeneration, loop exits after one pass; Phase 5 surfaces only should-fix/nit residue (or nothing). Same as the existing "reviewer finds nothing" edge case, now including the product-fit dimension in "nothing".
  • Loop hits the 2-iteration cap with a question still FAILing product-fit: the loop stops, the still-FAILing question is surfaced in Phase 5 as a top-of-batch "Kill or rewrite Q<id>?" with the rubric reason; the changelog shows the two rewrite attempts and why they didn't clear it. Author makes the call.
  • Author keeps a question the reviewer FAILed: recorded as Keep-with-reason in ## Dispositions; survey committed with the question; the Phase-9 summary notes the kept-against-advice item (parallel to the existing over-budget handling).
  • Survey uses a multi_field_open question and the chosen export platform doesn't support it natively: the transformer emits N open_short items + a section-header explanation; export/README.md lists the downgrade; an in-artifact comment marks it.
  • A convenience substrate asset is missing (e.g. serve.js or the turndown bundle): the skill warns once and skips the dependent extra (no preview server / no .md sidecar), continues; survey.html/preview.html are still produced.

Error Journeys

  • A hard-required substrate asset is missing (survey-preview.js, style.css, or viewer.js): the skill aborts at Phase 3.6 / Phase 7 with survey-design: missing substrate asset <path> — reinstall the pmos-toolkit plugin. It does not write a degraded survey.html/preview.html. The author reinstalls and re-runs.
  • Reviewer subagent returns malformed output (missing the product-fit verdict for some question, or no score): same handling as the existing per-question count mismatch — re-dispatch once naming the gaps; if still off, surface the gap, skip the loop's auto-regenerate for this run, and proceed to Phase 5 with whatever the reviewer did return (non-interactive: log and proceed).
  • The generator's loop-iteration regeneration produces an invalid survey.json (schema-invariant violation): the skill re-derives once; if still invalid, it abandons that iteration's regeneration, keeps the last-valid survey.json, notes it in the changelog, and exits the loop early to Phase 5.

Design Decisions

#DecisionOptions ConsideredRationale
D1Product-fit lives inside the existing survey-eval.md / question-eval.md artifacts (as their leading section), not a separate sub-pass with its own files.(a) separate product-fit.md artifact + extra reviewer dispatch; (b) fold into the two existing eval artifacts as their leading section(b) — user-confirmed. One reviewer dispatch, one place to look; product-fit findings lead so they're seen first. Avoids a third eval file and a second subagent round-trip.
D2Two scoring layers: a per-question binary PASS/FAIL product-fit verdict, plus a reviewer-wide weighted 0–100 score across all eval dimensions.(a) only binary per-question verdicts; (b) only a numeric score; (c) both — binary per question (clean kill/rewrite mapping) + a 0–100 dimension score as a loop progress signal(c) — user-confirmed. The binary verdict is what the author acts on per question; the 0–100 score gives the loop a progress signal ("72 → 91") and a way to prioritize which findings to fix first by point-weight. The score does NOT gate the loop (see D7).
D3The Phase-4 reviewer evaluates only; the generator owns every mutation to survey.json. Inside the loop, questions get auto-rewritten/killed by the generator (acting on the reviewer's recommended edits); the author still dispositions the residue in Phase 5 and sees a per-iteration changelog.(a) reviewer mutates the survey directly; (b) reviewer surfaces, author dispositions everything (no auto-fix); (c) reviewer evaluates + recommends; generator applies; bounded loop; author dispositions the residue(c) — user-confirmed. Single writer = the generator (the reviewer never writes questions — avoids self-grading theatre and split ownership); the bounded loop (D7) does the obvious fixes so the author isn't dispositioning duds; the changelog + residual-findings batch keep the author in control (can revert anything).
D4Missing substrate assets: two-tier — abort on hard-required (survey-preview.js, style.css, viewer.js), warn-and-skip-the-extra on convenience (serve.js, html-to-md.js, turndown bundle). No fallback renderer either way.(a) abort on any missing asset; (b) abort on hard-required only, warn-degrade on convenience; (c) warn-and-continue everywhere with a louder warning(b) — user-confirmed. Consistent with the skill's posture: abort when you can't deliver the contract (a usable preview.html/survey.html), warn when you can't deliver an extra (preview server, .md sidecar). A fallback renderer is a second code path to maintain for a broken-install case the author should just fix.
D5multi_field_open is added to the schema with a defined per-platform downgrade, rather than left as an ad-hoc extension or omitted.(a) omit it (author keeps extending ad-hoc); (b) add it with native-or-downgrade export mapping(b) — user wants it, but explicitly flagged the export concern; adding it without defining the downgrade would push the ad-hoc problem into the export phase. schema_version bump to 2 keeps it backward-compatible.
D6"Cosmetic-only" = severity nit AND a wording/title polish with no methodological or product-fit impact; such findings are listed, not asked.(a) drop cosmetic findings entirely; (b) keep them in the batch but sort last; (c) list them in a trailing line, still recorded in ## Dispositions(c) — the author still sees them (can act later) but isn't asked to disposition each; dropping them loses information, sorting-last still costs a click each.
D7The Phase-4 loop's exit condition is categorical, not score-gated: it stops when zero product-fit FAILs AND zero blocker-severity methodology findings remain, OR after 2 regeneration iterations — whichever first.(a) hard ≥90/100 score gate; (b) score gate confirmed twice / banded; (c) categorical conditions, score is a progress signal only(c) — user-confirmed. A single LLM-judge score near a hard cutoff is flappy (88 one run, 92 the next), making termination a coin flip; categorical conditions are deterministic. The 2-iteration cap bounds cost and context use regardless.
